<h1 id="tuma207-scenario-essay-mock-exam">TUMA207 Scenario Essay Mock Exam</h1>
<p>使用方式：每题按 12-18 分钟练习。不要背定义，按 <code>diagnosis -&gt; framework/tool -&gt; justification -&gt; action -&gt; monitoring</code> 写。</p>
<p>建议评分标准：</p>
<ul>
<li>20% 识别项目目标、约束和主要问题。</li>
<li>20% 识别 stakeholders / roles / decision rights。</li>
<li>25% 选择合适 PM tool/process，并说明为什么。</li>
<li>25% 给出可执行 actions、owners、metrics、communication。</li>
<li>10% 讨论 trade-offs、risks、assumptions。</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="question-1-ev-bus-conversion-program">Question 1: EV Bus Conversion Program</h2>
<p>Singapore plans to convert at least 60 ICE buses into EVs within two years. The authority wants to reduce transport emissions, avoid disruption to current bus operations, and support the long-term cleaner bus fleet target. You are the lead project manager.</p>
<p>Discuss how you would initiate and plan this program. Your answer should cover stakeholders, success criteria, risks, communication, and monitoring.</p>
<h3 id="answer-outline">Answer Outline</h3>
<ul>
<li>Reframe: public value + operational continuity + technical conversion。</li>
<li>Stakeholders: LTA, MOT, bus operators, commuters, contractors/OEMs, charging providers, NEA, depot managers, drivers, maintenance staff, residents。</li>
<li>Success criteria: 60 buses converted, no major service disruption, safety approval, emissions reduction, charging readiness, schedule within two years。</li>
<li>Approach: likely hybrid; predictive for compliance/construction/safety, adaptive for depot operations and stakeholder feedback。</li>
<li>Tools: project charter, stakeholder analysis, RACI, WBS/Gantt, risk register, communication plan, cost/schedule baseline。</li>
<li>Risks: charging capacity, conversion quality, procurement delay, driver training, operational disruption, budget overrun。</li>
<li>Monitoring: milestone reviews, SPI/CPI, defect/safety reports, service reliability, issue log, change request process。</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="question-2-plant-pals-landing-page-raci">Question 2: Plant Pals Landing Page RACI</h2>
<p>Trees Company Ltd is launching Plant Pals. The landing page requires six tasks: design page, create mockup, design image assets, write content, code page, and test page. Roles include Web Manager, Web Designer, Graphic Designer, Web Developer, QA Tester, and Content Writer.</p>
<p>Explain how you would assign RACI roles and why RACI improves project execution.</p>
<h3 id="answer-outline-1">Answer Outline</h3>
<ul>
<li>Explain RACI purpose: clarify work, decision rights, accountability。</li>
<li>Rule: one Accountable per task; at least one Responsible。</li>
<li>Suggested assignments:
<ul>
<li>Design page: Web Designer R, Web Manager A, Graphic Designer C, Developer/QA/Writer I。</li>
<li>Mockup: Web Designer R, Web Manager A, Developer/Writer/Graphic Designer C。</li>
<li>Image assets: Graphic Designer R, Web Designer or Web Manager A, others I。</li>
<li>Content: Content Writer R, Web Manager A, Web Designer C。</li>
<li>Code: Web Developer R, Web Manager A, QA/Web Designer C。</li>
<li>Test: QA Tester R, Web Manager A, Web Developer C。</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Trade-off: too many consulted slows decisions; too few causes rework。</li>
<li>Monitoring: task owner updates, issue log, final sign-off。</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="question-3-wonder-city-okr">Question 3: Wonder City OKR</h2>
<p>Wonder City expects population to double and job growth to rise by 48% in five years. WCTA will launch five new bus lines within two years. Routes must meet regulations, connect suburbs to downtown and public resources, serve at least 50% of densely populated areas, improve wait times, increase ridership, and gain community support.</p>
<p>Write OKRs and justify why they are effective.</p>
<h3 id="answer-outline-2">Answer Outline</h3>
<ul>
<li>Objective example: Provide reliable and convenient public transport that connects high-density suburbs to downtown and public resources within two years。</li>
<li>KRs:
<ul>
<li>Launch five compliant bus lines within 24 months。</li>
<li>Serve at least 50% of the most densely populated areas。</li>
<li>Complete community engagement and document feedback before route approval。</li>
<li>Reduce average waiting time after launch。</li>
<li>Increase ridership on target corridors within first year。</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Justification: measurable, time-bound, aligned with city objectives, not merely tasks。</li>
<li>Mention review/revision: OKRs can be updated as project progresses。</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="question-4-plant-pals-training-communication-plan">Question 4: Plant Pals Training Communication Plan</h2>
<p>The Plant Pals operations team must train employees over 10 days. Stakeholders include the core team, vendor, HR, Facilities, Print Shop, employees, your manager, and senior leadership.</p>
<p>Design a communication plan and explain why each communication method/frequency is appropriate.</p>
<h3 id="answer-outline-3">Answer Outline</h3>
<ul>
<li>Core team: daily interactive planning meetings; high involvement。</li>
<li>Vendor: one-time half-day meeting before SOW ends; transfer software/equipment knowledge。</li>
<li>HR: weekly/as-needed check-ins; supports training and staffing。</li>
<li>Facilities: weekly phone/in-person check-ins; rooms/equipment requirements。</li>
<li>Print Shop: planning meeting + weekly check-ins; manuals。</li>
<li>Employees: official weekly email + day-before reminders; instructions。</li>
<li>Manager: weekly high-level update; resource/escalation。</li>
<li>Senior leadership: concise email after training; outcome focus。</li>
<li>Mention push/pull/interactive logic。</li>
<li>Include owner, date/frequency, method, resource/notes。</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="question-5-plant-pals-test-batch-status">Question 5: Plant Pals Test Batch Status</h2>
<p>Two weeks into Plant Pals test shipments, customer survey data shows: 10% of plants are not properly potted; customer relations receives only 30% of requests due to software issue; delivery completion is 80% while target is 95%; some customers cancel subscriptions.</p>
<p>Write the logic of a project status report and choose a RAG status.</p>
<h3 id="answer-outline-4">Answer Outline</h3>
<ul>
<li>RAG: Amber because off-track but recoverable with targeted corrections。</li>
<li>Completed milestones: software installed, first test batch sent, orders exceeded target by 15%。</li>
<li>Upcoming milestones: newsletter by July 7, delivery 95% by July 19, error rate below 5%。</li>
<li>Issues:
<ul>
<li>Potting defects -&gt; complaints/profit loss -&gt; Warehouse Operations Manager -&gt; improve potting QA。</li>
<li>Software routing issue -&gt; dissatisfaction -&gt; IT Specialist -&gt; fix system/manual workaround。</li>
<li>Driver shortage -&gt; late delivery/cancellations -&gt; HR Specialist -&gt; hire temporary drivers/reassign capacity。</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Communication: sponsor high-level status; team issue log; owners/deadlines。</li>
<li>Monitoring: delivery rate, complaint capture rate, defect rate, cancellation rate。</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="question-6-vendor-sow">Question 6: Vendor SOW</h2>
<p>A vendor will configure supply chain management software, install software and fulfillment equipment, create training manuals and a maintenance guide, and meet HR/Training Manager. The vendor is not responsible for training employees or ongoing maintenance. Work should start no later than April 17 and finish within 10 business days. Payment occurs after completion.</p>
<p>Draft the main logic of the SOW and explain how it reduces procurement risk.</p>
<h3 id="answer-outline-5">Answer Outline</h3>
<ul>
<li>Purpose: enable Plant Pals operations/training readiness。</li>
<li>In scope: configure software, audit/optimize existing systems, install devices/equipment, create manuals/guide, knowledge transfer meeting。</li>
<li>Out of scope: employee training, ongoing maintenance, separate maintenance contract。</li>
<li>Deliverables: configured software, installed equipment, training manuals, maintenance guide, meeting record/sign-off。</li>
<li>Milestones: audit/configuration, installation, documentation/knowledge transfer。</li>
<li>Time: 10 business days, 80 hours if 8-hour workdays。</li>
<li>Payment: after delivery/acceptance。</li>
<li>Risk reduction: prevents scope creep, clarifies acceptance, supports vendor performance control。</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="question-7-evm-interpretation">Question 7: EVM Interpretation</h2>
<p>A software project has four phases, each budgeted at $10,000 and planned one after another. At the end of month 3, phase 1 is complete with $10,000 spent, phase 2 is complete with $12,000 spent, phase 3 is 50% complete with $9,000 spent, and phase 4 has not started.</p>
<p>Calculate PV, EV, AC, CV, SV, CPI, SPI and interpret the project status.</p>
<h3 id="answer-outline-6">Answer Outline</h3>
<ul>
<li>BAC = $40,000。</li>
<li>PV = $30,000 because three phases should be complete。</li>
<li>EV = $25,000 because phase 1 + phase 2 + 50% phase 3。</li>
<li>AC = $31,000。</li>
<li>CV = EV - AC = -$6,000。</li>
<li>SV = EV - PV = -$5,000。</li>
<li>CPI = 25/31 = 0.81。</li>
<li>SPI = 25/30 = 0.83。</li>
<li>Interpretation: over budget and behind schedule。</li>
<li>Actions: root cause analysis, forecast EAC/ETC, corrective actions, communicate status, change request if baseline needs revision。</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="question-8-project-closing">Question 8: Project Closing</h2>
<p>Plant Pals operations and training project is complete after six months. It reduced delivery cost, installed supply chain software, trained over 75% of employees, sent test batches, ran customer surveys, improved on-time delivery and customer service satisfaction, and launched operations. Some issues remain around future delivery performance and software maintenance.</p>
<p>Explain what should be included in the closeout report and why.</p>
<h3 id="answer-outline-7">Answer Outline</h3>
<ul>
<li>Executive summary: purpose, major milestones, outcome。</li>
<li>Key accomplishments: cost reduction, software/equipment, training, surveys, launch。</li>
<li>Lessons learned:
<ul>
<li>Went well: logistics cost reduction, survey feedback loop, training readiness。</li>
<li>Went wrong: software delay, potting defects, driver shortage; explain resolutions。</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Open items: reach 95% on-time delivery, ongoing maintenance contract, continue satisfaction tracking。</li>
<li>Next steps: support operations handover, future training schedule, maintenance/vendor plan。</li>
<li>Archive: final documents, timeline, acceptance records, resources。</li>
<li>Why: supports formal closure, knowledge transfer, future project improvement。</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="question-9-spotify-model-and-tailoring">Question 9: Spotify Model and Tailoring</h2>
<p>Your company wants to copy Spotify’s Squads, Tribes, Chapters, and Guilds because it is scaling software teams. Evaluate this decision using project management principles.</p>
<h3 id="answer-outline-8">Answer Outline</h3>
<ul>
<li>Explain model briefly。</li>
<li>Main argument: be inspired, do not copy blindly。</li>
<li>Link to tailoring, development approach, team culture, communication。</li>
<li>Autonomy vs alignment trade-off。</li>
<li>Assess context: team size, product modularity, organization culture, maturity, stakeholder dependencies。</li>
<li>Recommend pilot/experiment, feedback loops, gradual adaptation。</li>
<li>Conclusion: value is adaptability and continuous improvement, not labels。</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="question-10-multi-criteria-decision">Question 10: Multi-Criteria Decision</h2>
<p>A transport agency must choose among several technical solutions. Criteria include safety, cost, environmental impact, noise, maintainability, and user disruption. Explain how MCA can support the decision and what its limitations are.</p>
<h3 id="answer-outline-9">Answer Outline</h3>
<ul>
<li>Define alternatives and criteria。</li>
<li>Collect comparable data。</li>
<li>Score each alternative per criterion。</li>
<li>Weight criteria by priority/risk appetite。</li>
<li>Calculate weighted sum and rank。</li>
<li>Discuss sensitivity: different weights can change ranking。</li>
<li>Limitations: scoring and weighting include judgment; data may be uncertain; criteria units must be comparable。</li>
<li>Good answer links MCA to transparent decision-making, not absolute objectivity。</li>
</ul>
